Corgi

Cardigan Welsh Corgi

The Cardigan is a double-coated dog where the outer coat is dense, slightly harsh in texture, and of medium length.

The dog's undercoat is short, soft, and thick. The breed stands between 27–32 cm at the withers, and should weigh between 14–17 kg.

There are a greater number of colours of coat present in the Cardigan breed than the Pembroke, with the breed standard allowing for a variety of shades of red, sable and brindle.

History

The first recorded date for Corgis appearing in the show ring in Wales is 1925. Captain J. P. called together a meeting of breeders of both the Pembroke and the Cardigan varieties, and formed the Welsh Corgi Club, with an initial membership of 59 members. A general breed standard was drawn up, and Corgis began to appear in conformation shows.

Pembroke Welsh Corgi

Pembrokes feature pointed ears, and are somewhat smaller in stature than the Cardigan. They are low-set, intelligent, strong and sturdy with stamina sufficient to work a day on the farm. The common height at the withers is between 25–30 cm, while a male dog of this breed should weigh no more than 14 kg, and a female 11 kg. The tail is shorter than that of a Cardigan, which can be accomplished through breeding or docking.
